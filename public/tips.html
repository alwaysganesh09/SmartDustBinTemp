General Tips

The golden rules of recycling. Click to learn more!

Golden Rules

  • Clean & Dry: Rinse food containers and let them dry. One dirty item can contaminate an entire batch.
  • Know Your Local Rules: Recycling programs vary by city. Check your local municipality's website for specific guidelines.
  • Don't Bag Recyclables: Place items loose in your bin unless your local program specifically asks you to bag them.
  • When in Doubt, Throw it Out: It's better to discard a questionable item than to risk contaminating a batch of recyclables.

Paper & Cardboard

Paper Rules

  • Flatten Boxes: Break down all cardboard boxes to save space in bins and trucks.
  • Keep it Dry: Wet paper fibers are harder to recycle. Keep paper and cardboard out of the rain.
  • No Food Stains: Greasy parts of pizza boxes or food-soiled paper plates should be composted or thrown away.
  • Remove Embellishments: Staples and plastic windows are usually fine, but remove any plastic bags or bubble wrap.

Plastics

Know your numbers and what they mean. Click to find out.

Plastic Pointers

  • Check the Number: Look for the recycling symbol with a number (1-7). Most programs accept #1 (PET) and #2 (HDPE).
  • Caps On: It's now generally recommended to keep plastic caps on bottles and jugs. They are too small to be sorted alone.
  • No Plastic Bags: Do not put plastic bags or films in your curbside bin. Take them to a store drop-off location.
  • Empty & Clean: Make sure bottles and containers are empty and give them a quick rinse.

Glass

Glass Guidelines

  • Bottles & Jars Only: Only recycle glass food and beverage containers.
  • No Other Glass: Do not recycle window panes, mirrors, light bulbs, or ceramics. They have different melting points.
  • Remove Lids: Metal or plastic lids should be removed and recycled separately.
  • Color Separation: Some programs may require you to separate glass by color (clear, brown, green).

Metals

Aluminum and steel are infinitely recyclable. Click for tips.

Metal Must-Dos

  • Aluminum & Steel Cans: Both food and beverage cans are highly recyclable.
  • Clean Foil & Trays: Aluminum foil and trays can be recycled if they are clean of food residue. Ball them up.
  • Empty Aerosol Cans: Make sure aerosol cans are completely empty before recycling. Do not puncture them.
  • No Scrap Metal: Do not put large scrap metal items like pipes or hangers in your curbside bin.

E-Waste

Electronics need special handling. Click to see how.

Tech Trash

  • Never in the Bin: Electronics contain hazardous materials and should never go in your regular trash or recycling.
  • Find a Drop-off: Look for certified e-waste recycling centers, community collection events, or retailer take-back programs.
  • Batteries are Separate: Recycle batteries separately at designated drop-off locations. They can be a fire hazard.
  • Wipe Your Data: Before recycling computers or phones, be sure to erase all your personal data.
